Design Brief

In this project I was required to design and create a windpowered vehicle. It was to be powered by the force of wind as the final source of movement. There were no batteries allowed to be used at all in the product. It was to have a maximum width of 200mm and length of 300mm. I also had to hand in this folio of everything I did and my thought process.

Research

In this design the body is weak structurally but is also light making it move more with less force. It used the rubber band to wind up a propeller on the back of the vehicle which makes it move. The tension from the rubber band after being twisted causes the propeller to spin forcing the wind to push behind the vehicle making it move forward especially because it is very light weight it will go with more ease and further. They use a skewer and poked it through the bottle with bottle caps that are designed to be the wheels. This works well because there is a low resistance between the skewer and the plastic on the bottle causing it to spin well.
